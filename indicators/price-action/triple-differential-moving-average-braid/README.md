# Triple Differential Moving Average Braid

The Triple Differential Moving Average Braid weaves together three distinct layers of moving averages: short-term, medium-term, and long-term. Together, they provide a structured view of market trends across multiple time horizons.

This indicator is optimized for the 1D chart. For lower-timeframe analysis on 1H or 15m charts, it pairs naturally with the Granular Daily Moving Average Ribbon.

Unlike traditional moving average indicators that rely on a single crossover, this braid-style system incorporates both SMAs and EMAs. The dual-layer approach offers stability and responsiveness, helping traders read trend shifts, pullbacks, and structural transitions with greater clarity.

## Layers

The indicator consists of three layered moving average pairs:

1. Silver Thread: tracks immediate price momentum.
2. Royal Guard: captures market structure and developing trends.
3. Golden Section: defines major market cycles and overall trend direction.

Each layer is color-coded and dynamically shaded based on whether the faster moving average is above or below its slower counterpart.

## Silver Thread

The Silver Thread is the fastest-moving layer, built from the 21D SMA and 21D EMA.

The 21D SMA smooths recent price action and provides a baseline for short-term structure. The 21D EMA reacts more quickly to price changes, highlighting shifts in momentum.

When the SMA is above the EMA, price action remains comparatively stable. When the SMA falls below the EMA, short-term momentum may be weakening.

The Silver Thread often flips before the medium- and long-term layers. If it shifts bearish while the Royal Guard remains bullish, that may indicate a temporary pullback rather than a full trend reversal.

## Royal Guard

The Royal Guard uses the 50D EMA and 200D EMA to capture medium-term trend structure.

Because EMAs prioritize recent price action, this layer reacts faster than the Golden Section while still preserving broader trend context.

When the 50D EMA is above the 200D EMA, the market is in a confirmed medium-term uptrend. When the 50D EMA crosses below the 200D EMA, momentum has shifted bearish.

If both the Silver Thread and Royal Guard weaken together, the move may represent a deeper pullback or possible trend reversal.

## Golden Section

The Golden Section is the slowest and most stable layer, built from the classic 50D SMA and 200D SMA.

When the 50D SMA is above the 200D SMA, the market remains structurally constructive. When the 50D SMA falls below the 200D SMA, the broader structure is bearish.

Because SMAs weight past price data evenly, this layer moves more deliberately and helps filter temporary swings, false breaks, and short-term noise.

## Interpretation

When all three layers align in the same direction, the market is in a strong trending phase.

When the Silver Thread weakens but the Royal Guard and Golden Section remain constructive, the move may be a pullback inside a larger uptrend.

When the Silver Thread and Royal Guard both weaken while the Golden Section remains bullish, the market may be entering a medium-term correction.

When all three layers flip bearish, the structure may be signaling a larger trend reversal.

## Risk Notes

A narrowing of the shaded areas can suggest trend exhaustion or compression.

When the Golden Section remains bullish but the faster layers weaken, traders may watch for potential support zones or re-entry opportunities.

When all three layers flip bearish, traders may treat that as a warning that the prior trend structure has meaningfully changed.
